Technology, Media and Telecom

Our Technology, Media & Telecom (TMT) Law Practice supports businesses at the intersection of innovation, regulation, and commerce. We advise leading technology companies, media houses, telecom operators, startups, investors, and digital platforms on regulatory compliance, commercial transactions, and dispute resolution in India and globally.

Key Services

  • Technology
    • Advisory on IT, software licensing, SaaS, cloud, and data-driven business models.
    • Drafting and negotiating technology contracts, outsourcing agreements, and platform terms.
    • Advisory on data protection, cybersecurity, and cross-border data flows under Indian and global laws (GDPR, CCPA, DPDP Act 2023).
    • Legal support for fintech, edtech, healthtech, AI, blockchain, and emerging technologies.
  • Media & Entertainment
    • Drafting and negotiation of content production, licensing, and distribution agreements.
    • Advisory on OTT platforms, broadcasting regulations, and digital media policies.
    • Protection of intellectual property in film, television, music, gaming, and digital content.
    • Representation in defamation, IP infringement, and digital rights disputes.
  • Telecommunications
    • Regulatory advisory under the Indian Telegraph Act, TRAI regulations, and spectrum policies.
    • Support for licensing, spectrum allocation, and compliance for telecom operators.
    • Legal support for mergers, acquisitions, and joint ventures in the telecom sector.
    • Guidance on 5G, IoT, satellite communication, and digital infrastructure projects.
  • E-Commerce & Digital Economy
    • Structuring of online marketplaces and compliance with consumer protection and competition laws.
    • Advisory on cross-border digital trade, intermediary liability, and platform governance.
    • Guidance on advertising laws, influencer agreements, and digital marketing compliance.
  • Dispute Resolution
    • Representation in disputes relating to technology contracts, telecom regulation, and digital platforms.
    • Litigation before courts and sectoral regulators such as TRAI and TDSAT.
    • Arbitration and mediation in cross-border technology and media disputes.
